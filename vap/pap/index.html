Why PAP is Necessary

Current challenges in public AI systems and how PAP addresses them

Lack of Accountability

AI declares "ineligible" without explaining why. Citizens cannot even begin to appeal without understanding the basis for the decision.

Algorithmic Bias Entrenchment

AI trained on historically discriminatory data perpetuates and reproduces unfair decisions systematically.

Loss of Democratic Control

Unelected algorithms make decisions that affect citizens' fundamental rights, without democratic oversight or accountability.

Technical Design Principles

Temporal Fixation (Tiered)

High Assurance
IEEE 1588 PTP + microsecond timestamps
Practical Baseline
NTP/secure time source + millisecond + external timestamping

External Anchoring

Third-party verifiable integrity proofs

Examples: RFC 3161 TSA, public timestamping services, transparency-log style anchoring

Current System Deficiencies

No Decision Rationale Logs

Feature weights and scoring logic are not recorded for individual decisions

Mutable Timestamps

System clock dependent, no external anchoring or cryptographic proof

No Third-Party Verification

Cannot prove non-tampering to external auditors or courts

Privacy Protection & Data Minimization

Balancing transparency and privacy

The Dilemma

"We want to record everything for accountability"

â†•

"We want to minimize data for GDPR/privacy"

PAP's Solutions

1 Data Minimization
  • â€¢ Collect and record only minimum data needed for decisions
  • â€¢ Protected attributes not directly recorded; only aggregated for fairness monitoring
2 Anonymization/Pseudonymization
  • â€¢ Personal identifiers recorded as hashes
  • â€¢ Reverse lookup only under strict access control
3 Crypto-Shredding
  • â€¢ When right to erasure is exercised: destroy encryption key
  • â€¢ Hash chain maintained (audit integrity preserved)
  • â€¢ Personal data becomes unreadable
4 Differential Privacy
  • â€¢ Add noise to aggregate statistics
  • â€¢ Enable bias monitoring without identifying individuals
